Autism Family Guide

Clear, realistic information for families considering mHBOT as an optional add-on.

Adjunct wellness/recovery support. Results vary by individual. Not a substitute for medical care.

Current evidence position

Research for autism is mixed and inconclusive. Some families report changes, while reviews do not show consistent broad benefit across children.

mHBOT is not a replacement for speech therapy, occupational therapy, behavior support, school interventions, or doctor-led care.

If considered, keep it structured

  • Set one or two measurable goals
  • Continue evidence-based therapies
  • Track outcomes week by week

Family checklist

  • Have we defined realistic goals?
  • Do we understand costs and time commitment?
  • Are we screening safety and contraindications first?
  • Will we stop if outcomes are not meaningful?

Safety first

Children need close supervision, ear pressure guidance, and individual suitability screening before any session plan.

Practical next step

Book a parent consultation to review goals, suitability, and whether a short monitored trial is appropriate.

Positioning remains supportive and honest: optional add-on, realistic goals, and clear review checkpoints.
