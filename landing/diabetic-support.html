Diabetic Foot Wound Support

mHBOT can be discussed as supportive add-on care while core wound management remains primary.

Adjunct wellness/recovery support. Results vary by individual. Not a substitute for medical care.

How this is positioned

For diabetic foot wounds, oxygen-based methods may be explored as adjunct support in selected people. Afrateq provides mild-pressure sessions only (1.3-1.4 ATA).

This service does not replace wound clinics, glucose management, off-loading, infection care, vascular assessment, or doctor-led treatment plans.

Who usually asks about it

  • Adults with slower wound progress
  • People already under doctor/podiatry care
  • Families seeking an extra supportive option

What to expect

  • Safety screening before any booking
  • Series-based sessions rather than one-off assumptions
  • Progress review with your medical/wound-care team

Screening matters

Untreated pneumothorax and specific conditions or medications may exclude treatment until medically cleared.

Practical next step

Book a screening consultation and bring current wound-care notes, glucose context, and referral details where available.

Use mHBOT as one part of a broader, medically supervised care pathway.
