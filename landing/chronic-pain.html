Chronic Pain Support

A structured mHBOT adjunct option for people living with persistent pain.

Adjunct wellness/recovery support. Results vary by individual. Not a substitute for medical care.

What we communicate clearly

Response varies by pain pattern. Some people report functional improvement, while many require multimodal support and some see limited change.

mHBOT should sit inside a wider plan: medical review, movement therapy, sleep strategy, stress support, and medication plans where prescribed.

Potential support goals

  • Pain trend monitoring over a structured block
  • Daily function and activity tolerance
  • Support for participation in rehab plans

Who may consider this

  • Persistent pain despite standard approaches
  • Clients with physician support for adjunct care
  • People willing to track outcomes honestly

Tracking method

We focus on baseline-to-follow-up measures such as pain score trends, sleep, activity tolerance, and function markers.

Practical next step

Book a pain-support consultation and bring specialist notes or current treatment plans for context.

Goal: improve function and quality of life where meaningful benefit is seen.
